🍕 The Dough

How do I store the dough?

Keep it in the refrigerator until you're ready to use it.

  • Fresh dough: Best used within 2-3 days
  • Frozen dough: Thaw overnight in the fridge before using
How long should I let the dough rest before stretching?

Take the dough out of the fridge 1-2 hours before you plan to make pizza.

This lets it come to room temperature, which makes it much easier to stretch without tearing.

Pro Tip: If the dough keeps springing back when you stretch it, let it rest for another 10-15 minutes.
How do I stretch the dough?

Here's the technique:

  1. Flour your surface — Use semolina or regular flour to prevent sticking
  2. Press from the center — Use your fingertips to press outward from the middle, leaving the edges thicker for the crust
  3. Rotate as you go — Turn the dough as you stretch to keep it round
  4. Use gravity — Pick up the dough and let it hang, rotating it to let gravity stretch it evenly
  5. Don't use a rolling pin! — It pushes out all the air bubbles that make the crust light and airy
Pro Tip: It doesn't have to be perfectly round! Rustic shapes taste just as good.
My dough is sticky — what do I do?

Sticky dough is normal with high-hydration Neapolitan-style dough. Here's how to handle it:

  • Lightly flour your hands and work surface
  • Work quickly and confidently
  • Use a dough scraper to lift it if needed

A little stickiness is actually a good sign — it means the dough will be light and airy when baked!

What size pizza will the dough make?
  • Medium (250g): Makes a 12-14" pizza
  • Large (400g): Makes a 14-16" pizza

🔥 Baking

What temperature should I bake at?

450°F (230°C) works great for home ovens.

If you have a pizza stone or steel, preheat it in the oven for at least 30-45 minutes.

How long does it take to bake?

About 8-12 minutes depending on your oven.

Watch for the crust to turn golden brown and the cheese to bubble and get some brown spots.

What rack should I use?

Use the bottom rack for a crispier crust.

The heat from below helps create that perfect crispy bottom while the top gets bubbly and golden.

Pro Tip: Preheat a sheet pan or pizza stone on the bottom rack for an even crispier crust!
Can I use a regular baking sheet?

Yes! A regular baking sheet works fine. For best results:

  • Preheat the sheet in the oven
  • Lightly oil or dust with semolina before adding the pizza
  • Use the bottom rack
Do I need a pizza stone?

Not required, but it helps! A pizza stone or steel absorbs and radiates heat, giving you a crispier crust.

If you don't have one, a preheated baking sheet or cast iron pan works great too.

My bottom isn't crispy enough — what can I do?

For the crispiest crust, you want to preheat your cooking surface:

  • Pizza stone or steel — preheat for 30-45 min at 450°F (this is a game changer!)
  • Bottom rack — gets more direct heat from below
  • Pizza oven — if you have one, a quick reheat makes it perfect
Pro Tip: Get a wooden pizza peel! Build your pizza on the floured peel, then slide it directly onto the preheated stone or steel. This is how you get that restaurant-quality crispy bottom.

📦 The Kit

What comes in a pizza kit?

Each kit includes everything you need:

How long does the kit last?

For best results, use your kit within 2-3 days of pickup.

Keep everything refrigerated until you're ready to make pizza.

Can I add my own toppings?

Absolutely! The kit gives you a great base — feel free to add whatever you like.

Popular additions: mushrooms, olives, red onion, sausage, arugula (add after baking), hot honey.
